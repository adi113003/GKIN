01Persuasion techniques 4 found›

Loaded language

Emotive framing substitutes for evidence — “backfired,” “self-inflicted catastrophe,” “the architects of decline” — steering the reader toward a verdict the facts do not deliver.

False cause

A sequence is presented as causation: sanctions preceded the price spike, therefore sanctions caused it. The cited reporting names weather and pipeline flows as the primary drivers.

02Missing context 2 gaps›

Omitted baseline

The column never states pre-sanction energy prices, making the post-sanction figure read as a shock rather than a trend.

Counter-evidence dropped

Two cited outlets carried a competing explanation in the same week; neither is acknowledged.
