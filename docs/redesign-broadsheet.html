The Lead  ·  Media Forensics

Cut the spin. Keep the receipts.

A reading machine that won’t let a claim pass without naming its source — or admitting there isn’t one.

By the GKIN Desk Grounded verification Every verdict cited to a sentence

Paste an article, a URL, a screenshot, or a podcast. GKIN scores manipulation, names every persuasion technique, and ties every verdict to the exact source sentence that backs it — or tells you it can’t. Auditable, not a black box.

It does not hand you a confidence number and ask for trust. Each claim is checked against primary reporting, graded supported, contradicted, or insufficient, and shown beside the sentence that earned the grade. Where the record is silent, GKIN says so plainly rather than guessing.1

Built for desks that have to defend what they publish: the persuasion techniques are labelled, the missing context is itemised, and the manipulation index is a calm gauge, not a verdict in disguise.2

How it works  ·  Three steps
01

Paste anything

An article, a URL, a screenshot, or a podcast episode. Text, image, and audio all enter the same pipeline.

02

It checks the record

Claims are extracted and tested against primary reporting, then graded supported, contradicted, or insufficient.

03

You see the receipts

Every grade arrives with the exact source sentence behind it — or a plain note that the record can’t confirm it.
